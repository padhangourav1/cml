About the PI

Dr. Vinay Bulusu

I am a biochemist interested in understanding the metabolic organization and regulation of cellular processes in cancer and embryonic development. Our group uses a combination of functional genomics with advanced mass spectrometry, imaging, and computational modelling to understand metabolic cross-talk with signaling and gene transcription networks.

Our Research

While the role of thermodynamic and kinetic principles in organizing metabolic reactions are understood, the specific interplay of metabolites and enzymes in modulating signaling and gene transcription is now coming to the fore. We are focused on answering several key questions in this domain.

Metabolic Compartmentalization

In solid tumors, cancer cells rewire their metabolism to survive nutrient limitation. Our work revealed that under hypoxic conditions, acetyl-CoA pools are compartmentalized, channeling exogenous acetate directly into lipid synthesis, bypassing histone acetylation. We are pursuing further studies to understand such flux barriers.

Metabolites & Gene Expression

Metabolites like acetyl-CoA are crucial cofactors for enzymes that modify DNA and chromatin. We are developing novel, genetically encoded fluorescence-based sensors to visualize and understand their sub-cellular dynamics and impact on gene expression in real-time.

Metabolic Gradients

Altered metabolism creates metabolic gradients within tumors and developing embryos. We previously demonstrated a glycolytic gradient in the mouse embryo essential for axis elongation. We are dissecting the mechanisms of gradient formation and function in cancer and development.
